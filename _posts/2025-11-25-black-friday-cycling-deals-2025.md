**Tier 3 - Convenience:** 7. Smart trainer (winter training) 8. Tools and maintenance equipment 9. Bags and accessories

**Tier 4 - Nice to Have:** 10. Action camera 11. Premium clothing upgrades 12. Additional sensors

### Deal Verification Tips

**Check price history:**

- Use CamelCamelCamel for Amazon
- Check manufacturer's website prices
- Compare multiple retailers

**Beware fake discounts:**

- "50% off" might be fake MSRP
- Check reviews and ratings
- Verify seller reputation

**Calculate true savings:**

- Include shipping costs
- Consider return policies
- Factor in tax

## The Items Worth Full Price

Not everything needs to be on sale! Some items hold value:

**Worth full price:**

- Bike frames/complete bikes (rarely significant discounts)
- Custom-fit items (saddles, shoes if needed NOW)
- Emergency replacements (broken equipment)

**Wait for sales:**

- Clothing (always goes on sale)
- Accessories (frequently discounted)
- Previous-gen electronics (better deals later)

## Deals to AVOID

### Red Flags

**Skip these "deals":**

- Unknown Chinese brands with "90% off"
- Products with no reviews or all fake reviews
- "Limited time" deals that repeat weekly
- Bundles of low-quality items
- Heavily advertised "deal of the century"

**Quality over savings:**
Better to buy one quality item at 20% off than five garbage items at "80% off"!

## The Reality Check

### Do You NEED It?

Before buying, ask yourself:

- Will I actually use this?
- Do I already have something similar?
- Is this solving a real problem?
- Would I buy this at full price?

**The 20% rule:**
If it's not at least 20% off, it's probably not worth calling a "deal." And if you wouldn't buy it at 80% of full price, you don't need it at "50% off" either!
